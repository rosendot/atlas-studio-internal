# What You Own vs. What the Agency Manages

## What the Client Owns & Pays For Directly

These are accounts registered in the client's name, billed directly to the client. The agency is added as an admin for technical management purposes only.

| Item | Cost | Client Does | Agency Does |
|------|------|-------------|-------------|
| Domain name | ~$10–15/yr (Cloudflare Registrar) | Creates account, registers domain, pays renewal | Manages DNS records, added as account member |
| Email (Google Workspace) | ~$7/user/mo | Creates account, pays monthly bill | Sets up MX/DKIM/SPF records during onboarding, added as admin for troubleshooting |
| Google Analytics (GA4) | Free | Owns the property | Added as editor via Site Kit |
| Google Search Console | Free | Owns the property | Added as manager for SEO monitoring |
| Google Business Profile | Free | Owns the profile, responds to reviews | Added as manager for technical updates (hours, info, photos) |
| POS system (Square/Clover/Toast) | Varies | Owns account, manages menu & pricing, handles disputes | Connects website to POS API – nothing else |

---

## What the Agency Owns & Manages

These are agency infrastructure and intellectual property. They are shared across all clients and are not transferred.

| Item | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Hosting (Cloudways/DigitalOcean) | Client site lives here as part of the retainer. Not transferable. |
| Custom WordPress theme | Agency intellectual property. Built for the client, not licensed to them. |
| Plugin licenses | Rank Math, Wordfence, UpdraftPlus, and others are on agency accounts. Do not transfer. |
| Backup storage | Off-site backups exist while the client is active. A final backup can be provided on exit. |
| GitHub repositories | All theme code and integrations are in private agency repos. Not transferred. |
| SMTP relay (SendGrid/Mailgun) | Transactional email routes through agency account. |
| MainWP dashboard | Internal agency tool for monitoring and updates. Not client-facing. |

---

## What the Agency Does NOT Manage

Anything that touches the client's customers or money directly is the client's responsibility.

- Menu pricing, item descriptions, or modifiers in their POS
- Payment disputes or chargebacks
- Responding to Google or Yelp reviews
- Writing or sending emails to their customers
- Social media accounts (feeds can be displayed on the site; the accounts are theirs)
- Email marketing (Mailchimp, Klaviyo, etc.)
- Google Ads or any paid advertising
- POS hardware issues
- Any decisions requiring access to their banking or payment processor

---

## What Happens If the Client Leaves

| Item | Outcome |
|------|---------|
| Domain | They keep it – registered and paid for in their name |
| Email | Unaffected – billed directly to them |
| GA4 / Search Console / GBP | Unaffected – owned by them, agency admin access removed |
| POS account | Unaffected – nothing to do with hosting |
| Website content | Provided as a content export (text, media) on request |
| Custom theme | Not transferred – agency intellectual property |
| Plugin licenses | Not transferred – agency licenses |
| Hosting | Site taken offline or migrated at client's expense |
| Backups | Final backup provided on request; ongoing backups stop |

---

## Summary for Client Conversations

> "You own everything that matters – your domain, your email, your Google accounts, your POS. I get added as an admin so I can do my job without you having to call me every time something needs a technical change. Your monthly retainer covers the hosting, the website itself, security, updates, and ongoing maintenance. If you ever move on, you keep everything in your name and I step out cleanly."
